MY TEACHING PHILOSOPHY

  • I believe children have the most confidence in learning when the classroom environment is calm, relaxed, and full of fun educational activities that enhance interest in learning and exploring.
  • Young students need an educational facilitator rather than an assessment dictator.
    Children should feel safe making mistakes. Learning at this age should be messy and
    exciting!

DISCIPLINE PHILOSOPHY

We keep an uplifting and positive environment. I will always redirect and explain undesirable behaviors as a learning moment. Children at this age must be exclusively taught appropriate behaviors through consistency and patience. To keep the “calm” classroom atmosphere, I will never raise my voice at any child in response to a behavioral issue. Tantrums are somewhat normal for this age group, and I will make sure the child and classmates are safe during a meltdown. If this way of disciple isn’t a good fit for your scholar, or has excessive tantrums that continue to disrupt learning and fun for the other children, you will be promptly notified.

CLASSROOM ATMOSPHERE

  • With my teaching background, the classroom atmosphere accidentally became a mix between a Montessori class and a traditional in home “daycare” or Preschool. I am sensitive to visual overstimulation, so the classroom is decorated with that in mind. The classroom is calm and relaxed, but still fun! We are a “homeschool” so it feels like dropping your child off to family, but with the confidence that they will be ready for Kindergarten! We also love nature and spending time exploring in our backyard, so the classroom extends beyond the “class” daily

HEALTH AND SAFETY

  • We are an extremely small class which makes thorough cleaning and sanitation much easier.
  • We sanitize daily and have a professional cleaning team sanitize our classroom as well as the entire home weekly (Wednesdays)
  • Children who are sick may not attend class (including my own)
  • Temps will be checked every morning upon arrival.
  • We are required by the state to have monthly fire drills and safety precautions.
  • No one outside of my family is ever allowed in the classroom without proper background and health checks.
  • I can not release a child to anyone who isn’t on the pick up list.
  • I am a mandated reporter, any issues I notice pertaining the safety of my students, I must report.
